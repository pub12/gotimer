# Outreach Email & Message Templates

**Date:** 2026-05-17
**Use:** Drop-in templates for the contacts named in `2026-05-17-growth-outreach-plan.md`. Each is annotated with **when to use**, **what to customize**, and **what NOT to do**.

---

## General principles

1. **Lead with the recipient's work, not yours.** First sentence should reference something specific they published or said recently. If you can't find that, you're targeting the wrong person or you haven't done enough research.
2. **One ask per message.** Listicle inclusion OR podcast guest OR review — never bundled.
3. **Concrete differentiator, not a generic pitch.** "It's free" is not a differentiator. "Shareable URL with the Søberg protocol pre-configured" is.
4. **No attachments, no signup links, no unsubscribe boilerplate.** This is a 1:1 email, not marketing.
5. **Reply window.** Wait 7-10 days before a follow-up. Don't follow up twice — move on.
6. **Subject line discipline.** Specific and concrete. Never "Hi" or "Quick question" or "Idea for [their site]."

## Template 1 — Listicle inclusion (EdTech / classroom)

**When to use:** Larry Ferlazzo, TCEA TechNotes, Educators Technology, WeAreTeachers, Facilitator School, The Fountain Institute, Workshop Amigo.

**Subject:** Free classroom toolkit (no signup) — for your "[exact list name]" post

> Hi [first name],
>
> Your "[exact post title]" still ranks #[N] for "[the search term]" — it's the page I found when I went looking for a [tool type] last month.
>
> I built [tool name] at gotimer.org/classroom and would love to be considered for the next refresh. Three things that are a bit different from the tools currently on your list:
>
> 1. **[Concrete differentiator]** — e.g., "No signup or login at all. Class lists save to localStorage, so teachers can bookmark a URL with their class baked in."
> 2. **[Concrete differentiator]** — e.g., "No ads on the tool pages themselves."
> 3. **[Concrete differentiator]** — e.g., "All four classroom tools (name picker, group generator, noise meter, tally) on a single hub URL — so a teacher only has to remember one address."
>
> No expectation either way — happy to keep building regardless. If you have a few minutes to take a look, the hub is gotimer.org/classroom and the name picker specifically is at gotimer.org/classroom/name-picker.
>
> Thanks,
> [name]

**Customize:**
- Recipient's first name (always check spelling)
- Exact post title and current search-rank position (use Search Console or a private incognito search)
- The search term you used to find them
- Three differentiators specific to *their* existing list (don't repeat tools they already have)

**Don't:**
- Don't attach screenshots.
- Don't include "for SEO purposes I'd be happy to backlink."
- Don't follow up if no reply after 10 days. They saw it.

## Template 2 — EdTech newsletter pitch (Tony Vincent / Eric Curts / Monica Burns)

**When to use:** Replying to their most recent newsletter, OR cold email if you can't get on the list quickly enough.

**Subject:** Re: [exact newsletter subject line, if replying]

> Hi [first name] —
>
> Loved this week's pick of [specific tool they featured] — I hadn't seen [specific feature of that tool] before.
>

The [specific differentiator] is the bit I'm most curious whether it's actually useful for teachers — would love your gut take if you have a minute.
>

>

**Customize:**
- Reference a tool they featured in the *most recent* issue, not an old one.
- One concrete differentiator only.
- The "no newsletter slot ask" line is real — *do not* ask in the first email. If they reply positively, *then* ask in the follow-up.

**Don't:**
- Don't say "love your newsletter" (everyone does).
- Don't ask for a feature in the first message. Build the relationship.
- Don't reply on a different newsletter than the one you're referencing.

## Template 3 — Podcast guest pitch (Vicki Davis / Kasey Bell / Jake Miller / Holly Clark)

**When to use:** Pitching as a guest on an established EdTech or facilitation podcast.

**Subject:** Guest pitch — "[specific episode angle]" for [podcast name]

> Hi [first name],
>
> I've been listening to [podcast name] since [specific episode that resonated — name it]. The way you handled [specific topic] in that one was the reason I started thinking differently about [related topic].
>
> Pitching a guest segment that I think fits your format:
>
> **Angle:** "[Specific, narrow episode angle, not a topic]"
> **Why now:** [Something timely — e.g., "with state testing windows opening in March, classroom timer use spikes 4x"]
> **Why me:** I built gotimer.org, which has [specific stat — e.g., "8 classroom tools used by ~X teachers/week, based on traffic"]. I've spent the last [time period] talking to teachers about what they actually need from these tools.
>
> Specifically NOT pitching a generic "tell us about your tool" segment. The angle I'd want to cover is "[specific takeaway listeners would learn]."
>
> If it's not a fit, no worries — just figured I'd ask directly rather than try to come in through a PR firm.
>
> [name]

**Customize:**
- Specific episode + what you took from it. If you haven't actually listened, **don't send this**.
- Specific, narrow episode angle — not a topic. Bad: "EdTech for math teachers." Good: "Why the green/yellow/red signaling system from competitive debate works in middle-school math classrooms."
- One specific takeaway listeners would actually learn.

**Don't:**
- Don't pitch yourself as the expert. Pitch the *episode angle*.
- Don't pitch "tell us about your tool." Hosts hate this.

## Template 4 — Coach cold email (debate / Toastmasters)

**When to use:** Cold-emailing 10-15 named speech & debate coaches at high schools (emails are public on school sites).

**Subject:** Free debate timer with [specific format] preset — for [school name] team

> Hi Coach [last name],
>
> I came across the [school name] [team name — Lincoln-Douglas team / Speech & Debate squad] roster on your school page and noticed you compete in [specific format, e.g., Public Forum and LD].
>
> I built a free web timer at gotimer.org/productivity/debate-timer with format presets for PF, LD, Policy, WSDC, Parli, and Toastmasters. Two things specific to coaches that I think matter:
>
> 1. **Custom-preset URLs** — if your district uses non-standard speech times, I can build a URL that bakes them in (e.g., gotimer.org/productivity/debate-timer?format=district-tx-pf) so your team uses the same one every round.
> 2. **Projection mode** — full-screen green/yellow/red stoplight for in-person tournaments. Tested on a standard classroom projector at 1920x1080.
>
> No cost, no signup, no ads — I'm not selling anything. If it's useful for your next tournament, I'd be grateful for any feedback. If you'd like the custom-preset URL, just reply with your district's speech times and I'll send a link back.
>
> Thanks for what you do,
> [name]
> gotimer.org

**Customize:**
- School name + team name (check the team page).
- Specific format(s) they compete in.
- "Coach [last name]" — never "Hi there" or first-name to a coach you haven't met.

**Don't:**
- Don't email coaches at competing schools in the same district within 24 hours (word travels fast and looks spammy).
- Don't include analytics tracking pixels (coaches block them).
- Don't follow up more than once.

## Template 5 — Subreddit modmail (r/Sauna, r/Toastmasters, r/Debate, r/Twitch)

**When to use:** Before posting any tool announcement. Standard practice in mature subreddits.

**Subject:** Tool post — would this fit r/[subreddit]?

> Hi mods,
>
> I built a free web tool that's relevant to this community and wanted to check in before posting rather than guess at your self-promo rules.
>
> Tool: [tool name + URL]
> What it does: [one sentence]
> Why it fits this sub: [one sentence — reference a specific common ask or pinned post]
> Self-promo context: I'm the builder; the tool is free with no ads, signup, or upsell. I'll include the disclosure in the post.
>
> Two questions:
> 1. Is this OK to post as a standalone, or should I share it in an existing thread (e.g., "[specific weekly thread name]")?
> 2. Is there a particular day or format you prefer for tool posts?
>
> Happy to skip if it's not a fit.
>
> Thanks,
> [Reddit username]

**Customize:**
- Reference a specific weekly thread or pinned post.
- "I'm the builder" — always disclose.

**Don't:**
- Don't modmail and then post the same day. Wait for a reply.
- Don't follow up on modmail. Mods are volunteers.

## Template 7 — Direct reply to a forum/Reddit "looking for a timer" thread

**When to use:** Search finds a thread where someone explicitly asked for the kind of tool you just shipped. **This is the warmest possible outreach context — don't waste it with a sales pitch.**

> Hi — saw your post asking about [exact thing they asked for]. I happen to have built something close to what you described: [URL].
>
> Specifically for your use case ([quote a phrase from their post]): it does [specific thing they need] via [how it works]. If it's not quite right, happy to tweak it — I built it solo and the next feature priority comes from messages exactly like yours.
>
> No signup, no ads, no upsell. Full disclosure: I'm the builder.
>
> [optional: 1-line offer of a custom config]

**Customize:**
- Quote a phrase from their post (proves you read it).
- Address their specific use case, not generic features.

**Don't:**
- Don't copy-paste this across multiple threads on the same day (Reddit mods will see it as spam).
- Don't post in old threads (>6 months). Use Reddit chat to DM the OP instead.
- Don't mention SEO, traffic, or "checking out my project."

## What NOT to send

Examples of pitches that will get filtered, ignored, or block-listed:

- "Hi! Just wanted to share my new tool..."
- "I'm a fan of your work, love what you do..."
- "Quick question — do you accept guest posts?"
- Any email with attachments under 10MB (immediate spam flag in many filters).
- Any email with tracking pixels (educators block them aggressively).
- "I noticed your site doesn't have [tool category] — would love to fill that gap."
- Any pitch with a calendar booking link before the recipient has agreed to a call.
